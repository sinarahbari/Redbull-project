The back story of Red Bull's 1st motorsports athlete, F1's Gerhard Berger

Today,becoming a Red Bull-sponsored athlete can be a career-changing moment for motorsports athletes, and it all started with Austrian F1 legend Gerhard Berger in 1989.It wasn't just the considerable natural ability behind the wheel of a Formula One car (which earned him 10 Grand Prix wins with three different teams) that endeared Gerhard Berger to fans, rival drivers, and the sport's officials alike. In F1's ultra-competitive world he also possessed an easy charm, charisma, and fun-loving nature that made him seem a bit of a delightful throwback, too. So, it seemed a natural fit in 1989 when Red Bull decided that the Austrian driver should be taken on as their first sponsored athlete in a landmark deal. As Red Bull's figurehead driver, Berger's winning personality made him an instant success, but that easy-going nature and reputation as one of F1's great pranksters concealed just what a steely and courageous competitor he was something that was graphically illustrated in that very year.

2022 F1 Esports - Race Eleven


A rainy day at Interlagos sees Frede bag another podium

As rain descended on Interlagos, The Bulls lined up on the grid ready to take on the penultimate race of the 2022 F1 Esports Pro Championship, with Frede in P5 and Marcel in P11. In a race much less chaotic than round ten at Suzuka, The Bulls kept their heads down to deliver solid drives. Frede took yet another podium, managing to snag P2 in the closing laps of the race, while Marcel narrowly missed out on point again, finishing where he started in P11. As lights out approached, so did the rain, but both Bulls kept their cool and perfected the race start. Frede held position while Marcel found himself immediately battling to keep his place, momentarily dropping to P14 but fighting his way back to P12 all before the end of the first lap. While Carreton, Idowu and Leigh continued to battle it out behind him, Marcel kept out of trouble and charged on.
